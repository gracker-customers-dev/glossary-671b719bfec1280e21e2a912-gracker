British Standard 7799

A standard for information security management systems.

Description

British Standard 7799 (BS 7799) was a benchmark for establishing, implementing, maintaining, and continually improving an information security management system (ISMS). It was published in two parts: Part 1 provided a code of practice for information security management, while Part 2 outlined the requirements for an ISMS. The standard aimed to help organizations manage their information security risks effectively, ensuring the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of sensitive information. BS 7799 was widely adopted in the UK and influenced the development of the international standard ISO/IEC 27001, which is now the globally recognized framework for information security management. The standard emphasized the importance of risk assessment, management commitment, and continuous improvement in the face of ever-evolving security threats. Organizations sought certification against BS 7799 to demonstrate their commitment to information security and to reassure stakeholders about their practices in safeguarding information assets.

Examples

  • Adoption of BS 7799 by organizations seeking to improve their information security practices.
  • Transition of BS 7799 to ISO/IEC 27001 for better global recognition.

Additional Information

  • BS 7799 was first published in 1995 and later revised in 2005.
  • It served as a precursor to ISO/IEC 27001, which was published in 2005.
