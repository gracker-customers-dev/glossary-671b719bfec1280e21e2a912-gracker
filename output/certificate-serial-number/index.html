certificate serial number

A unique identifier assigned to a digital certificate by the issuing certificate authority (CA).

Description

In the context of digital certificates, a certificate serial number is a unique identifier that is assigned to each certificate by the certificate authority (CA) that issues it. This serial number is critical for the identification and management of digital certificates within a Public Key Infrastructure (PKI). The serial number ensures that each certificate can be uniquely distinguished from others, even if they have the same subject (the entity to which the certificate is issued). It plays a crucial role in the validation process during certificate verification, allowing clients and servers to check if a certificate is still valid or has been revoked. The serial number is included in the certificate's data structure and is typically represented as a hex string. It is also important in the context of Certificate Revocation Lists (CRLs) and Online Certificate Status Protocol (OCSP), where it helps identify certificates that should no longer be trusted.

Examples

  • A certificate with serial number '1234567890ABCDEF' issued by a CA.
  • A revoked certificate might have its serial number listed in a CRL for clients to check.

Additional Information

  • Serial numbers are usually assigned sequentially or randomly by the CA.
  • They can be used to track and manage certificates effectively in enterprise environments.
