association

A relationship established between devices in a network, often used in the context of wireless networks.

Description

In network security, the term 'association' primarily refers to the relationship established between a client device and an access point (AP) in wireless networks. When a device attempts to connect to a Wi-Fi network, it must first authenticate itself to the access point. This process involves the exchange of credentials and may include additional security protocols like WPA2 or WPA3. Once authentication is successful, the device and the AP establish an association, allowing the device to send and receive data over the network. This association is vital for ensuring that communication is secure and that unauthorized devices cannot access the network. Furthermore, the association includes parameters like the association ID, which uniquely identifies the connection between the client and the AP, and may also involve encryption keys that protect the data transmitted over the air. Proper management of associations is critical to maintaining network security and performance, as it helps prevent unauthorized access and ensures that only legitimate devices are allowed to communicate within the network.

Examples

  • A smartphone associating with a home Wi-Fi router after entering the correct password.
  • A laptop establishing an association with a corporate access point after successful authentication using a digital certificate.

Additional Information

  • Associations can be managed through protocols such as 802.11 for wireless networks.
  • Security measures like EAP (Extensible Authentication Protocol) can be used during the association process to validate user credentials.
