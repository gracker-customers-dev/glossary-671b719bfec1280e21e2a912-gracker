X.509 public-key certificate

A digital certificate that uses the X.509 standard to associate a public key with an identity.

Description

An X.509 public-key certificate is a widely used digital certificate format that is part of the X.500 series of standards. It serves as a means to establish a secure connection and authenticate identities over networks, particularly in public key infrastructure (PKI). The certificate contains information about the identity of the certificate holder, the public key, the digital signature of a trusted certificate authority (CA), and the validity period of the certificate. X.509 certificates are used in various security protocols, including SSL/TLS, which secure web communications. When a user connects to a secure website, their browser checks the website's X.509 certificate to verify the site's identity and ensure that the public key contained in the certificate belongs to the site. This verification process helps to prevent man-in-the-middle attacks and unauthorized access. The X.509 standard has evolved over time, with versions including X.509 v1, v2, and v3, with v3 being the most commonly used due to its support for extensions that enhance functionality and provide additional features.

Examples

  • SSL/TLS certificates used by websites to secure HTTPS connections.
  • Email certificates (S/MIME) used to sign and encrypt email communications.

Additional Information

  • X.509 certificates can be self-signed or signed by a trusted Certificate Authority.
  • They often include extensions for additional functionalities such as key usage and enhanced validation.
