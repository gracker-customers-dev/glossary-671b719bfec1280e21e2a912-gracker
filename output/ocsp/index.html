OCSP

Online Certificate Status Protocol.

Description

OCSP, or Online Certificate Status Protocol, is an Internet protocol used for obtaining the revocation status of an X.509 digital certificate. It is widely used in network security as a way to determine whether a certificate is still valid or has been revoked by the issuing Certificate Authority (CA). OCSP allows clients to query a CA's OCSP responder to check the status of a specific certificate in real-time, rather than relying on traditional methods such as Certificate Revocation Lists (CRLs), which require downloading and maintaining a potentially large list of revoked certificates. OCSP provides a more efficient and timely way to handle certificate validation, especially in environments where certificates are frequently issued or revoked. The response from an OCSP server indicates whether the certificate is good, revoked, or unknown, along with a timestamp to assure the client of the response's freshness. This protocol is essential in establishing secure communications over the Internet, particularly in SSL/TLS scenarios, where the integrity and validity of certificates are crucial for maintaining trust.

Examples

  • A web browser uses OCSP to check if the SSL certificate of a website is still valid when establishing a secure connection.
  • An email client utilizes OCSP to verify the status of a digital signature certificate before accepting a signed message.

Additional Information

  • OCSP responses can be signed by the CA to ensure authenticity and integrity.
  • Some implementations also support OCSP stapling, where the server includes a recent OCSP response with its certificate to improve performance and privacy.
