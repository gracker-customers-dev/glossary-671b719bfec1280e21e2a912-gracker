denial of service

A denial of service (DoS) attack is an interruption in an authorized user's access to a computer network or service.

Description

A denial of service (DoS) attack aims to make a machine or network resource unavailable to its intended users by overwhelming it with a flood of illegitimate requests or exploiting vulnerabilities. This can be achieved through various methods, including sending excessive traffic to the target system, exploiting software bugs, or using a botnet, where many compromised devices are used to conduct the attack simultaneously. The result is that legitimate users experience delays, interruptions, or complete inability to access the services or resources they need. DoS attacks can be especially damaging for businesses, leading to loss of revenue, damage to reputation, and potential legal implications if customer services are affected. Variants of DoS attacks include Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS), where the attack is launched from multiple systems, making it more challenging to mitigate. Organizations often implement various security measures, such as firewalls, intrusion detection systems, and rate limiting, to help defend against these types of attacks.

Examples

  • A botnet flooding a website with traffic to take it offline.
  • An attacker exploiting a vulnerability in a network device to crash it.

Additional Information

  • DoS attacks can target any service, including web servers, email servers, and DNS servers.
  • Mitigation strategies may include traffic filtering, redundancy, and the use of content delivery networks.
