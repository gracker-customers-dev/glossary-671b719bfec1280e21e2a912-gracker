electronic commerce

The buying and selling of goods and services over the internet.

Description

Electronic commerce, commonly known as e-commerce, refers to the transactions conducted via the internet, encompassing a wide range of business activities. E-commerce includes retail shopping, online auctions, and payment services, allowing consumers and businesses to conduct transactions seamlessly from anywhere at any time. It is facilitated by various technologies such as mobile commerce, electronic funds transfer, supply chain management, and internet marketing. In the context of network security fundamentals, e-commerce raises particular concerns regarding the protection of sensitive information, such as credit card details and personal data. Businesses engaged in e-commerce must implement robust security measures to safeguard their platforms against cyber threats like data breaches, phishing, and malware attacks. Secure Socket Layer (SSL) encryption, secure payment gateways, and compliance with regulations such as the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) are crucial for maintaining customer trust and ensuring safe online transactions.

Examples

  • Amazon.com - An online retail giant offering a vast range of products.
  • eBay - A popular platform for online auctions and consumer-to-consumer sales.

Additional Information

  • E-commerce can be categorized into B2B, B2C, C2C, and C2B models.
  • The rise of mobile commerce has significantly influenced consumer purchasing behaviors.
