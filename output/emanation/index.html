emanation

The unintended release of data or signals from a device that can be captured and analyzed.

Description

In the context of network security, emanation refers to the unintentional transmission of electromagnetic signals from electronic devices, which can be intercepted and exploited by unauthorized entities. These signals can carry sensitive information, including data being processed by a computer or information transmitted over a network. The phenomenon is a concern for security professionals, as attackers can use various techniques, such as side-channel attacks or electromagnetic eavesdropping, to capture these emanations. Emanations can occur from various devices, including computers, printers, and wireless communication systems. The potential for data leakage through emanation highlights the importance of implementing countermeasures, such as shielding and secure design practices, to protect sensitive information in environments where confidentiality is critical. Understanding emanation is essential for ensuring the integrity and security of communications, particularly in government, military, and corporate settings where sensitive data is frequently handled.

Examples

  • Electromagnetic signals emitted from a computer's CPU that can be captured by a nearby receiver.
  • Radio frequency emanations from a wireless router that can be intercepted to gain unauthorized access to a network.

Additional Information

  • Emanation security is a field focused on preventing information leaks through electromagnetic emissions.
  • Countermeasures include the use of TEMPEST-certified equipment, which is designed to limit emanation.
