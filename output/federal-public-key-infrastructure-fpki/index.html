Federal Public-key Infrastructure (FPKI)

A framework that provides a secure method for the creation, management, distribution, and revocation of digital certificates for federal agencies.

Description

The Federal Public-key Infrastructure (FPKI) is a comprehensive framework established to facilitate secure electronic communication and transactions within and between federal agencies in the United States. It employs a hierarchical structure of trusted Certificate Authorities (CAs) that issue digital certificates, which are used to validate the identities of individuals, organizations, and devices. FPKI ensures the integrity, confidentiality, and authenticity of data exchanged over networks by providing cryptographic tools that support public key cryptography. The infrastructure is designed to comply with federal standards and policies, enabling interoperability among various federal entities. This makes it essential for applications such as secure email communications, electronic signatures for official documents, and secure access to government services. By providing a standardized approach to identity management and trust establishment, FPKI plays a critical role in enhancing the security posture of the federal government, thereby protecting sensitive information from unauthorized access and cyber threats.

Examples

  • Use of FPKI for secure email communications within federal agencies.
  • Implementation of digital signatures for official documents using FPKI services.

Additional Information

  • FPKI is part of the broader U.S. government strategy for cybersecurity.
  • It supports compliance with various regulations such as FISMA (Federal Information Security Management Act).
