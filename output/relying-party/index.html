relying party

A relying party is an entity that relies on the information or assertions provided by another entity, typically in the context of authentication and identity management.

Description

In the realm of network security and identity management, a relying party (RP) is an organization or system that accepts and uses the identity information or assertions provided by an identity provider (IdP). The RP relies on the IdP to authenticate users and provide accurate user information, thereby allowing the RP to make decisions regarding access control and user privileges. This relationship is crucial in frameworks such as Single Sign-On (SSO) and federated identity systems, where users can authenticate once and gain access to multiple services without needing to provide credentials repeatedly. The trust relationship between the RP and the IdP is established through protocols and standards such as SAML (Security Assertion Markup Language) or OAuth, which define how assertions or tokens are shared and verified. By leveraging an IdP, RPs can enhance security, reduce the burden of password management, and provide a better user experience while ensuring that sensitive information is handled appropriately.

Examples

  • An online banking website that trusts a third-party identity provider to authenticate users.
  • A corporate intranet that uses a centralized authentication service to verify employee identities.

Additional Information

  • Relying parties must carefully assess the security practices of their identity providers to mitigate risks.
  • The relationship between RPs and IdPs is often governed by Service Level Agreements (SLAs) to ensure compliance and reliability.
