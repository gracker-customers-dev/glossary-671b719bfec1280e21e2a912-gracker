authority revocation list (ARL)

A list of certificates that have been revoked by a certificate authority (CA).

Description

An Authority Revocation List (ARL) is a critical component in network security, particularly within Public Key Infrastructure (PKI). It is issued by a Certificate Authority (CA) and contains identifiers for digital certificates that are no longer valid before their scheduled expiration dates. Such revocation can occur for various reasons, including the compromise of the private key associated with the certificate, changes in the affiliation of the certificate holder, or the certificate being issued in error. The ARL is used by systems to ensure they do not trust certificates that have been revoked, thus maintaining the integrity and security of communications. When a user or system encounters a certificate, it checks the ARL to determine if the certificate is still valid or has been revoked. The timely distribution and updating of ARLs are essential for maintaining secure communications in networks to prevent unauthorized access and data breaches.

Examples

  • A digital certificate for a company employee who has left the organization has been revoked and listed in the ARL.
  • An SSL certificate for a website that was found to be compromised is included in the ARL to prevent users from trusting it.

Additional Information

  • ARLs can be distributed via various methods, including online distribution points or through CRLs (Certificate Revocation Lists).
  • The regular update of ARLs is crucial to ensure that systems have the latest information on which certificates are revoked.
