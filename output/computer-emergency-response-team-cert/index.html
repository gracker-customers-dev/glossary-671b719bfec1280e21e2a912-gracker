computer emergency response team (CERT)

A group of experts that provides support and guidance in the event of computer security incidents.

Description

A Computer Emergency Response Team (CERT) is an expert group that responds to computer security incidents and provides assistance to organizations in managing and mitigating cyber threats. CERTs are typically established to help organizations understand, prepare for, and respond to various cybersecurity incidents, including data breaches, malware infections, and denial-of-service attacks. They offer a wide range of services, including incident analysis, vulnerability assessment, security training, and the development of best practices for cybersecurity. CERTs also work in collaboration with law enforcement, government agencies, and private sector partners to share information about threats and vulnerabilities. They play a crucial role in improving the overall security posture of the organizations they serve by promoting awareness and providing guidance on incident response strategies. By analyzing incidents and disseminating information about emerging threats, CERTs help to foster a safer and more resilient cyber environment for all stakeholders.

Examples

  • The U.S. Computer Emergency Readiness Team (US-CERT) provides resources and incident response services to federal agencies and the public.
  • The European Union Agency for Cybersecurity (ENISA) supports member states in establishing national CERTs and enhancing their capabilities.

Additional Information

  • CERTs may be public or private entities and can vary in size and scope based on their mission.
  • They often conduct research on cybersecurity threats and contribute to the development of security standards.
